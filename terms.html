2. Scope of Analysis — What the App Does and Does Not Read

This is one of the most important things to understand about DecodeFood. We are completely honest about where our analysis starts and stops.

2.1 What DecodeFood Analyzes

DecodeFood analyzes the Ingredients List section of a food label only. This includes:

  • Ingredient names captured via on-device OCR (camera scan)
  • Ingredient names retrieved from third-party barcode databases (Open Food Facts API and USDA Branded Foods API)
  • Ingredient names entered manually by the user

2.2 What DecodeFood Does NOT Currently Analyze

DecodeFood does not currently parse, interpret, or analyze the following sections of a food label, even if they are visible in a camera scan:

  • "Contains:" statements (e.g., "Contains: Wheat, Milk, Soy")
  • "May Contain:" statements (e.g., "May Contain: Peanuts")
  • "Allergen Information:" sections
  • "Processed on equipment that also handles..." statements
  • "Manufactured in a facility that also processes..." statements
  • Nutrition facts panels
  • Serving size or quantity information
  • Expiration dates or lot codes
⚠ Critical — Severe Allergy Users: Cross-contamination warnings ("May Contain", "Processed in a facility") are NOT analyzed by DecodeFood. If you have a severe or life-threatening food allergy, you MUST read the full physical packaging label yourself and must not rely solely on the App for cross-contamination safety.

3. Third-Party Data Accuracy

We use the best available data sources, but we are honest that no database is perfect. This is why we always encourage you to read the physical label.

When you scan a product barcode, DecodeFood retrieves ingredient data from the Open Food Facts database (a crowd-sourced, non-profit third-party service) and, as a fallback, the USDA Branded Foods API (a public U.S. government database). DecodeFood has no control over the accuracy, completeness, or currency of data in either database.

Third-party ingredient data may contain errors, omissions, outdated information, or product variants that differ from the physical product you are holding. DecodeFood does not independently verify third-party data.

When you scan a label using the camera OCR feature, accuracy depends on the quality of the image, lighting conditions, label font, label damage, and the clarity of the printed text. OCR technology may misread, skip, or incorrectly parse some ingredients.
